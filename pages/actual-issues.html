Issue #1: THE PRIMARY CONSTRAINT - Operating Room Throughput

üéØ Root Causes:

  • Surgeon Variability: Procedure time ranges from 28-62 minutes. No standardization of technique, tool selection, or sequencing. Senior surgeons perform 1.5x faster than junior surgeons.
  • OR Setup Delays: Average 22-minute gap between surgeries for cleaning, instrument sterilization, and setup. No assembly line mentality or parallel preparation.
  • Patient Positioning & Prep: Time spent on patient positioning, draping, and anesthesia administration averages 18 minutes. No pre-staging done in holding area.
  • Surgical Variability: No documented best practice protocols. Surgeons use different instrument sequences based on personal preference rather than optimized order.
  • Communication Gaps: OR staff doesn't proactively prepare for next patient based on surgeon's needs. Last-minute requests cause delays.

üìå Theory of Constraints Connection:

The operating room is the CONSTRAINT in ClearSight's system. It has the least flexibility and longest lead time to expand. Every minute of unused OR time is irreplaceable revenue loss. The constraint must be optimized first and protected from depletion.

Issue #2: Pre-Authorization & Insurance Processing Blockage

üéØ Root Causes:

  • Manual Authorization Process: Each authorization request is manually entered into insurance company portals. No integration or automation. Duplicate data entry across multiple systems.
  • Incomplete Patient Information: Many initial submissions lack required information, requiring follow-up calls. 25% of rejections are due to missing or incorrect data.
  • Insurance Company Response Time: Insurers take 2-5 business days to respond even for routine cases. No expedited pathways for urgent cases.
  • No Pre-Authorization Planning: Surgeries are scheduled before pre-authorization is confirmed, leading to cancellations. Poor visibility into authorization status creates bottleneck.
  • Limited Staff for Authorization: Only 1 FTE dedicated to authorization processing for 3,000 annual surgeries. Workload is high relative to manual process complexity.

üìå Theory of Constraints Connection:

Insurance authorization is a DEPENDENT CONSTRAINT that prevents patients from reaching the OR. Fixing the OR constraint is ineffective if patients can't be scheduled. This is an example of how non-physical constraints (process, policy) can be just as limiting as equipment constraints.

Issue #3: Workforce Morale & Capability Gap

The Vicious Cycle of Staff Burnout

üéØ Root Causes:

  • Understaffing & Burnout: Staff levels are 15% below recommendations. Staff works frequent overtime, leading to fatigue and errors. Turnover creates perpetual understaffing cycle.
  • Inadequate Training: New OR staff receive only 2 weeks of orientation. Senior staff training is inconsistent. No continuous skill development program for technique optimization.
  • Compensation Gap: ClearSight pays 12-15% below market rate for surgical nurses and technicians compared to hospital competitors.
  • Poor Scheduling Predictability: Shift schedules change frequently, making work-life balance difficult. Staff can't plan personal time reliably.
  • No Career Development: Limited advancement opportunities. No clear pathway for technicians to become senior technicians or specialty roles.
  • Lack of Feedback & Recognition: Performance reviews are annual and superficial. No recognition system for staff exceeding targets or suggesting improvements.

üìå Theory of Constraints Connection:

Workforce constraints are POLICY CONSTRAINTS created by management decisions. Unlike equipment (which has physical limits), people constraints can be rapidly solved through proper incentives, training, and working conditions. This represents high-ROI improvement opportunities.

Issue #4: Infection & Quality Control Deficiencies

üéØ Root Causes:

  • Inadequate Sterilization Protocols: Sterilization verification uses outdated processes. No real-time monitoring. Instruments occasionally don't meet sterility standards but are used anyway due to time pressure.
  • Insufficient Post-Op Monitoring: Post-op calls only occur for 40% of patients. No systematic follow-up protocol. Complications detected late, increasing severity.
  • Environmental Control Issues: OR air quality management is inconsistent. Humidity and particle count not always within surgical standards.
  • Staff Training on Asepsis: Inconsistent adherence to aseptic protocols. New staff not adequately trained on proper technique.
  • Equipment Maintenance: Surgical equipment maintenance is reactive (repair after failure) rather than preventive. Instrument calibration not done on schedule.

üìå Theory of Constraints Connection:

Quality issues are SUBORDINATE SYSTEM CONSTRAINTS. Quality control should be optimized to support, not hinder, the main constraint (OR throughput). Current infection rates require surgery cancellations/delays, further reducing OR throughput.

Issue #5: Billing & Revenue Cycle Leakage

Revenue Leakage Pipeline

üéØ Root Causes:

  • Billing Error Rate: 18% of claims have errors on first submission - coding errors, patient info mismatches, missing documentation. Requires rework and resubmission.
  • Denied Claim Rate: 6.8% denial rate is 2-3x higher than benchmarks. Many denials are recoverable with proper appeal process but require significant effort.
  • Collections Process Lag: 52-day average to collect payment. Outstanding receivables exceed $8.2M. Not converting completed work into cash quickly.
  • Manual Billing Process: Billing staff manually codes procedures, enters claims, and follows up on denials. 30% of time spent on low-value rework and re-entry.
  • Pre-Authorization Pricing Uncertainty: Patients often discover after surgery that services weren't fully pre-authorized or approved at quoted price. Leads to payment disputes and write-offs.

üìå Theory of Constraints Connection:

In TOC, throughput is defined as sales minus truly variable costs. Revenue that isn't collected is equivalent to revenue not generated. Billing delays and denials directly reduce throughput and are a system constraint requiring attention.

Summary: The Web of Interconnected Constraints

ClearSight's constraints form an interconnected system that cannot be solved in isolation:

Constraint Type Description Example
Primary Constraint Most limiting factor - determines system throughput Operating Room capacity & efficiency
Dependent Constraints Feed work to the primary constraint Pre-auth, patient scheduling, workforce availability
Subordinate Constraints Must support without hindering primary Quality control, infection prevention
Policy Constraints Self-imposed limitations by management Compensation levels, training budget, scheduling rules

Key Insight: Addressing only one constraint without considering the system will be ineffective. Theory of Constraints provides a systematic framework to:

  • Identify the primary constraint limiting throughput
  • Exploit it (maximize its effectiveness)
  • Subordinate all other resources to support it
  • Elevate the constraint (remove it or expand capacity)
  • Repeat the cycle for the next constraint
Path Forward: The detailed strategy in the "Strategy" section applies TOC's 5-step focusing process to systematically address ClearSight's constraints. By following this methodology, ClearSight can increase patient volume by 54%, improve profitability by 185%, and become the highest-performing cataract center in its market.
