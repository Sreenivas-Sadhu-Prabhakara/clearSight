Dr. Connors, the Chief Medical Officer of ClearSight, identifies the following business and operational challenges that are constraining the organization's growth and profitability.

1. Surgical Suite Bottleneck

Operating rooms are not being fully utilized. While we have the capacity to perform 6,000 surgeries annually, we're only achieving 3,000 due to inefficient scheduling and excessive setup times between procedures.

2. Extended Procedure Duration

Average cataract surgery takes 45 minutes, but our best performers can do it in 32 minutes. The 13-minute gap represents a 41% productivity loss per surgery. This is driven by surgeon variability, poor OR preparation, and inefficient instrumentation sequencing.

3. Pre-Operative Process Inefficiency

Our pre-op assessments take an average of 90 minutes per patient. Insurance pre-authorizations cause 15% of surgeries to be postponed or cancelled. Patient intake paperwork is repetitive and often contains errors requiring rework.

4. Workforce Instability & Morale Issues

Staff turnover exceeds 28% annually, compared to healthcare industry average of 16%. Surgical nurses and technicians report burnout due to understaffing, lack of proper training, and insufficient recognition. This creates a vicious cycle of poor service quality and patient dissatisfaction.

More Challenges Identified

5. Material & Instrument Management Deficiency

Instruments are not properly tracked. Emergency re-sterilization is required 8% of the time due to misplaced instruments, causing costly delays. Inventory management is manual and error-prone, leading to stockouts or overstock situations.

6. Insurance Authorization Delays

45% of authorizations take longer than 48 hours. Some cases require manual follow-up and re-submission. This delays surgery scheduling by an average of 5-7 days and results in 8-12% surgery cancellations due to patient frustration.

7. Quality Control & Infection Prevention

Post-operative complication rates are 6.2%, above the 3% benchmark. Infection rates are 2.1% compared to best-in-class 0.8%. Root cause analysis shows inadequate sterilization protocols and insufficient post-op monitoring.

8. Revenue Cycle Management

Average days to collect payment is 52 days (industry standard: 35 days). Billing errors occur in 18% of cases, requiring correction and resubmission. Denied claims average 6.8% of monthly revenue and take an average of 45 days to resolve.

9. Patient Communication Breakdown

No-show rate is 12%. Patient satisfaction scores are only 82%. Patients report confusion about pricing, post-op instructions, and follow-up appointments. Communication gaps exist between clinical and administrative teams.

10. Technology & Data Integration Issues

Multiple legacy systems don't communicate with each other. Clinical records, billing, and scheduling systems are siloed. Staff spends 15% of their time on data re-entry and cross-system lookups instead of patient care.

Dr. Connors' Assessment Summary

"ClearSight has excellent clinical talent and infrastructure, but we're leaving substantial revenue on the table due to operational inefficiencies. We're operating at 65% capacity when we could be at 90%+. The combination of surgical delays, pre-op bottlenecks, and workforce instability creates a constrained system where our theoretical maximum throughput far exceeds our actual output."

"We need to apply systematic problem-solving methodology to identify and break these constraints. The question is: what is the single most limiting factor preventing us from achieving our full potential? And how do we subordinate all other decisions to removing that constraint?"
