Goldratt's Theory of Constraints Framework

üéØ Core Principle

Every system has at least one constraint that limits its throughput. Management's role is to identify this constraint and subordinate all other decisions to it, maximizing system throughput.

5Ô∏è‚É£ The Five Focusing Steps

  1. IDENTIFY the constraint(s) limiting system performance
  2. EXPLOIT the constraint to maximize its capacity
  3. SUBORDINATE all other resources to the constraint
  4. ELEVATE the constraint through investment or process redesign
  5. REPEAT the process for the next emerging constraint

The Root Cause

ClearSight's cataract surgery center operates like a production line where each patient must pass through sequential stages. The OR Scheduling bottleneck acts as a dam, creating a 42-day backup that prevents 250 patients monthly from receiving surgery. This constraint limits throughput to just 50% of available capacity, costing the center $250K in monthly margin.

The Solution Path

By applying Goldratt's Theory of Constraints, we can systematically eliminate this bottleneck through: Exploiting current scheduling processes, Subordinating all other activities to OR utilization, and Elevating the constraint through targeted investments in scheduling technology and additional OR capacity.
